Mosquito & Tick Control

Reclaim Your Yard This Season

Mosquitoes and ticks aren't just annoying — they're vectors for serious diseases. Mosquitoes transmit West Nile virus, Zika, and Eastern Equine Encephalitis. Ticks are the primary vector for Lyme disease, Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever, and Ehrlichiosis. Protecting your family starts in your own backyard.

Partners Pest Solutions offers barrier spray programs that dramatically reduce mosquito and tick populations on your property, so you can use your outdoor spaces without the constant threat of bites.

What We Target

  • Mosquitoes (Aedes, Culex, and Anopheles species)
  • Deer ticks / blacklegged ticks (primary Lyme disease vector)
  • American dog ticks
  • Lone Star ticks
  • Brown dog ticks

Barrier Spray

Technicians treat foliage, shrubs, grass lines, and shaded areas where mosquitoes and ticks rest during the day. Residual protection lasts 3–4 weeks.

Season Program

Monthly treatments from April through October provide continuous protection through peak mosquito and tick season.

Source Reduction

We identify and recommend elimination of standing water sources — the breeding ground for mosquitoes — as part of every visit.

Special Events

One-time treatments available for weddings, parties, and outdoor events. Book 48 hours in advance for best results.

How the Barrier Program Works

Our technicians perform a thorough yard inspection, then apply a residual barrier treatment to the areas where mosquitoes and ticks are known to harbor — particularly shrub borders, wood lines, ornamental plantings, and shaded grass. The treatment begins working within minutes and provides residual protection for 3–4 weeks.

For maximum effectiveness, we recommend a monthly program starting in early spring when populations begin to emerge. Consistent treatments throughout the season reduce the breeding population and prevent the mid-summer spike that makes outdoor living miserable.

Safe for Families & Pets

Treatments are applied to outdoor surfaces only. Allow the application to dry completely (typically 30–60 minutes) before allowing children and pets back into the treated area. All products are applied in strict accordance with label requirements by licensed technicians.
