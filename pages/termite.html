Termite Control

Don't Let Termites Eat Your Investment

Termites cause billions of dollars in structural damage every year — and most homeowners don't know they have an infestation until significant damage has already occurred. Unlike most pests, termites work silently inside walls, floors, and structural timbers where they're invisible to the untrained eye.

Partners Pest Solutions provides thorough termite inspections and proven treatment options to eliminate active colonies and protect your property long-term.

Types of Termites We Treat

  • Subterranean termites (most common and destructive)
  • Formosan subterranean termites
  • Drywood termites
  • Dampwood termites

Warning Signs

Mud tubes along foundation walls, hollow-sounding wood, discarded wings near windowsills, bubbling or peeling paint, or visible frass (termite droppings).

Treatment Options

Liquid soil termiticide barrier (Termidor), termite bait stations, wood treatments, and localized foam injection for drywood infestations.

Inspection

Comprehensive inspection of interior and exterior including crawl spaces, attic access, garage, and foundation perimeter with written findings report.

Real Estate Reports

We provide WDO (Wood-Destroying Organism) inspection reports required for many real estate transactions.

Liquid Barrier Treatment

The most common and highly effective method. A continuous treated zone is created around your foundation by trenching and injecting termiticide into the soil. Subterranean termites pass through the zone and carry the active ingredient back to the colony, eliminating it at the source.

Bait Station Systems

Termite bait stations are installed in the soil around your property at regular intervals. Termites discover and feed on the bait, then share it with the colony. Stations are inspected and maintained on a scheduled basis and provide ongoing monitoring — even after the colony is eliminated.

Annual Termite Inspections

Even if you've never seen evidence of termites, an annual inspection is one of the best investments a homeowner can make. Early detection means lower treatment costs and far less structural damage. Ask about our annual inspection and monitoring programs.
